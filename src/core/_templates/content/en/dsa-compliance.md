**4. Nature of the Service**

%SITE_NAME% is an online platform that provides digital services and content. Users may create accounts, access content, and interact with the platform's features.

**5. Content Moderation**

**5.1 Automated moderation**

We employ automated content moderation tools to detect content that may violate our [Terms of Service](/terms-of-service) or applicable law. Automated tools are used to:

a) Detect and prevent prohibited content;
b) Detect hate speech, incitement to violence, and other prohibited content;
c) Flag content for human review where automated systems identify potential violations.

**5.2 Human moderation**

Content flagged by automated systems is reviewed by trained human moderators. Decisions that significantly affect users (such as account termination) are always reviewed by a human.

**5.3 Grounds for content moderation decisions**

Content moderation decisions are based on:
a) Applicable EU and national laws;
b) Our [Terms of Service](/terms-of-service);
c) Our [Community Guidelines](/community-guidelines);
d) Fundamental rights considerations, including freedom of expression.

**6. Reporting Illegal Content**

If you encounter content you believe to be illegal under EU or national law, you may report it by contacting us at %CONTACT_EMAIL%. For the full reporting procedure, including what to include in your report and how we process it, see our [Content Removal and Complaints Policy](/content-removal-and-complaints).

**7. Statement of Reasons**

When we restrict content or a user's account, we provide a clear and specific statement of reasons, including:

a) The facts and circumstances relied upon;
b) The legal or contractual ground for the decision;
c) Where applicable, a reference to the automated detection method used;
d) Information about the possibility to appeal the decision.

**8. Complaints and Appeals**

Users have the right to appeal any content moderation decision. Appeals are reviewed by a person who was not involved in the original decision. For the full complaints and appeals procedure, see our [Content Removal and Complaints Policy](/content-removal-and-complaints).

If you are not satisfied with the outcome of your appeal, you may refer the matter to a certified out-of-court dispute settlement body in accordance with DSA Art. 21.

**9. Transparency**

In accordance with DSA Art. 15, we publish annual transparency reports covering:

a) The number of orders received from EU Member State authorities and actions taken;
b) The number of reports received through our reporting mechanisms and actions taken;
c) Content moderation activities, including the use of automated tools;
d) The number of complaints received through our internal complaint-handling system and their outcomes;
e) The median time to process reports and complaints.

**10. Illegal Content Reporting to Authorities**

In accordance with DSA Art. 18, where we become aware of information giving rise to a suspicion that a criminal offence involving a threat to the life or safety of a person has occurred, is occurring, or is likely to occur, we promptly inform the law enforcement or judicial authorities of the Member State(s) concerned.
